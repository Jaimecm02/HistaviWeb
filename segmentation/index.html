Aortic Root Segmentation

Imaging Analysis Framework

Our sophisticated segmentation methodology represents a cutting-edge approach to analyzing cardiovascular morphology through precise medical imaging techniques. By focusing on high-resolution CT scans captured before and six months after Transcatheter Aortic Valve Replacement (TAVR), we create a powerful diagnostic tool for understanding complex aortic root deformations and post-procedural anatomical changes.

Research Objective

Develop a computational method for precise anatomical characterization and quantitative analysis of aortic root structures before and after interventional procedures.

Anatomical Segmentation Strategy

Our meticulous segmentation process employs advanced computational techniques to precisely isolate and extract critical cardiovascular structures with high accuracy:

Targeted Anatomical Structures

  • Aortic Root: Primary focus for detailed morphological analysis
  • Left Ventricle: Comprehensive assessment of ventricular geometry
  • Left Atrium and Mitral Valve: Detailed evaluation of atrial structures
  • TAVR Device: Precise localization and characterization of implanted device

Segmentation Methodology

After structure isolation, our advanced model implements a strategic sectioning approach. The model is precisely cut, leaving a minimal margin beneath the TAVR device to optimize visualization. This technique strategically exposes the critical conduction system located in the membranous septum, providing unprecedented insights into cardiac microarchitecture.

Patient Selection Criteria

Inclusion Parameters

  • Pre-Surgical Screening: Absence of Mitral Clip Procedure (MCP) prior to intervention

    Ensures minimal procedural interference and baseline anatomical integrity

  • Artifact Minimization Protocol: Stringent quality control for TAVR and MCP-related imaging artifacts

    Guarantees high-fidelity image reconstruction and analysis

  • Visualization Optimization: Prioritize exceptional visualization of Transapical Surgical Valve Implantation (TSVI)

    Enables comprehensive assessment of procedural outcomes

  • Image Integrity Verification: Validation of pre-implantation CT image completeness

    Eliminates datasets with significant artifacts or discontinuities

  • Cardiac Phase Standardization: Fixed cardiac phase at 75% of cardiac cycle

    Ensures consistent comparative analysis across different patient datasets
