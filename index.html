About the Histavi Project

The Histavi Project investigates the relationship between structural deformations caused by Transcatheter Aortic Valve Replacement (TAVR) and the subsequent impact on the cardiac conduction system.

Our research focuses on quantifying these deformations using advanced imaging and computational techniques to predict and potentially prevent complications that lead to permanent pacemaker implantation.

Methodology

An approach to quantifying and analyzing deformations in the aortic root structure following TAVR procedures.

Segmentation

High-resolution CT scans are processed to identify and isolate the aortic root structures before and after TAVR implementation.

Learn more

Registration

Pre and post-intervention models are precisely aligned using registration techniques, allowing for accurate comparison of structural changes.

Deformation Analysis

Advanced computational methods quantify the deformation for different heights and sections of interest of the aortic root.
